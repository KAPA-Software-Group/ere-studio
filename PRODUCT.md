# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Prospective private clients (residential), hospitality and commercial developers, design press, and collaborators (architects, contractors, fabricators) evaluating the studio's point of view before reaching out. They land on the site after a referral or a publication; they scroll slowly, read project metadata closely, and judge the studio by the quality and confidence of the work shown. Mobile usage is real but the long-form evaluation happens on desktop.

## Product Purpose

ERE Studio is an interior and spatial design practice. The website is the primary instrument for converting a curious visitor into a project conversation. Success looks like: a visitor who arrives unsure and leaves convinced the studio is operating at architectural caliber, not at the level of a residential interior decorator. Every page should demonstrate calm authority and material sensitivity through how it is composed, not by claiming it.

## Brand Personality

Editorial, cinematic, warm. Three words: composed, cinematic, considered. The voice is sparing and concrete; copy never restates a heading. Where copy is present, it carries the weight of a magazine caption, not a marketing tagline. The brand should feel like a monograph of completed work rather than a sales surface.

## Anti-references

- Generic AI-flavored SaaS marketing pages (Inter + purple gradients + glassmorphism + hero-metric grid).
- "Modern interior designer" Squarespace templates (sans-serif headlines, full-bleed slideshow, beige-everything, identical card grids of project tiles).
- Editorial-typographic reflex lane: italic display serif + small uppercase tracked labels + ruled separators repeated as section grammar, with no imagery. This is the closest trap for the current direction; the architecture-firm density and real imagery push us out of it.
- Drop-cap-and-broadsheet magazine pastiche. We borrow editorial weight from cinematic photography and dense project metadata, not from typography costume.

## Design Principles

1. **Show, do not announce.** Imagery and project metadata do the convincing. Copy steps aside.
2. **Architectural density.** Lists of project facts (year, scope, location, type, materials, collaborators) are part of the visual texture, not afterthoughts. Dense data, generously spaced.
3. **Cinematic pacing.** Long scroll, deliberate rhythm, large image moments that earn the room they take. Motion is slow, exponential, and never interrupts a read.
4. **Burgundy as ink, not decoration.** The accent is used the way an architect uses a single red line on a section drawing: rare, deliberate, structurally meaningful. Not as decoration spread across surfaces.
5. **Drawing-room precision.** Alignment, hairlines, and grid discipline read as drafted, not "designed." Every misalignment is intentional; nothing accidental survives.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA baseline. Burgundy `#5A1A2B` on white meets 4.5:1 for body and large text. All motion must respect `prefers-reduced-motion`; entrance reveals and parallax collapse to instant. Focus rings are visible against both white and burgundy surfaces (currently underspecified — to be added in the bolder pass). All interactive elements meet 44×44px minimum touch target.
